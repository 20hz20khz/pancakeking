"You can do this," he said. He untied his tie and started over. "You are
the pancake king. Everyone know it. You're unstoppable." He pulled the tie
through the knot. It was too short this time. He untied it again and started
over. "Nobody flips pancakes better than the pancake king," he told his
reflection. "Nobody." He had no idea, but these would be his last words.
A few minutes later, he was driving the snow-covered route to the regional
pancake flipping competition.
